Multi-physics

Elastocaloric cooling

Elastocaloric cooling is a promising technology that utilizes the temperature change of a material caused by an applied mechanical stress to produce cooling. The technology has the potential to provide a more energy-efficient and environmentally friendly alternative to traditional vapor compression refrigeration systems.

Magnetism

iron-loss

Iron-loss, also known as core loss, refers to the energy loss that occurs in the core of an electrical transformer due to the interaction of the alternating magnetic field with the magnetic properties of the iron core. The topic has been widely studied for more than a century, and it remains an active area of research due to its importance in the design and operation of electrical power systems.
