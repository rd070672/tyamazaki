Elastocaloric cooling is a cooling technology that utilizes the thermodynamic properties of certain materials known as elastocaloric materials to transfer heat. These materials, when subjected to mechanical stress, exhibit a change in temperature, known as the elastocaloric effect. The technology holds promise as an environmentally friendly and energy-efficient alternative to conventional cooling methods such as vapor compression systems.

Historical Overview

The concept of elastocaloric cooling was first proposed by Lord Kelvin in 1852, who described the idea of using the elastic energy of a material to produce cooling effects. However, it wasn't until the early 2000s that the technology began to gain traction, with the development of new materials and techniques that made it possible to produce a significant cooling effect. In recent years, the technology has been the subject of intense research, with many groups working to improve its efficiency and develop new materials that can be used for elastocaloric cooling.
